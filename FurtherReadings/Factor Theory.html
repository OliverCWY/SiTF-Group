Is Factor Theorm capable to find all the real roots for integral equations?

Factor Theorm is a theory to factorize polynomials and solve integral eqations that students learn in Year 3.

For any polynomial `P(x)=ax^n+bx^(n-1)+...+z`, if `P(k)=0`, where `k in {r/s:r,s in ZZ, r|z, s|a}`, `(x-k)` is a factor of `P(x)` and vise versa.
The question now is: can it give us all the real roots of any integral equation?

Surprisingly, yes, but why?

[Proof]
For a eqation `ax^n+bx^(n-1)+...+z=0`, we divide it by `a`.
Then we get `x^n+b/ax^(n-1)+...+z/a=0`.
Considering a rational root of this eqation, `r/s` where `r,s in ZZ and (r,s)=1`, we can transform the eqation into `(x-r/s)(x^(n-1)+Bx^(n-2)+...+Z)=0`.
`Z` can be expressed as `p/q` where `p,q in ZZ and (p,q)=1`, as it is a rational number.
By inspection, `r/s*Z=z/a`, meaning that `(r*p)/(s*q)=z/a`.
Since `r, p, s, q, z, a` are all integers and `(r,s)=(p,q)=1`, `r*p` is a factor of `z` and `s*q` is a factor of `a`.
Hence, `r|z and s|a`. (Proven)
