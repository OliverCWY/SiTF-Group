Why do cubic graph have rotational centers?

A cubic graph is a graph which equation is in the form `y=ax^3+bx^2+cx+d`, where `a!=0`.
In secondary 3, you will learn that all cubic graphs have a rotational center each, but why?

[Hint]
To prove that a graph has a rotational center, we can show that every point on the graph can find another point on the graph to form a segment so that all the segments formed have the same midpoint.
[Proof]
For any cubic graph `y=ax^3+bx^2+cx+d`, let `M` be `(m,n)`, `P` be `(e,f)` and `Q` be `(p,q)`,where `e+p=2m` and `f+q=2n`, `P` and `Q` are on the cubic graph and `x in RR`.

`f+q`
`=ae^3` `+be^2` `+ce` `+d` `+ap^3` `+bp^2` `+cp` `+d`
`=ae^3` `+be^2` `+ce` `+d` `+a(2m-e)^3` `+b(2m-e)^2` `+c(2m-e)` `+d`
`=(2b+6ma)e^2` `-(12m^2a+4mb)e` `+8m^3a` `+4m^2b` `+2mc` `+2d`
`n`
`=(b+3ma)e^2` `-2(3m^2a+mb)e` `+4m^3a` `+2m^2b` `+mc` `+d`
`=(b+3ma)e^2` `-2m(b+3ma)e` `+4m^3a` `+2m^2b` `+mc` `+d`
For `M(m,n)` to be a fixed point, let `m=-b/(3a)`, then `n` is a constant.

Hence for every point `P` on the graph of `y=ax^3+bx^2+cx+d`, there is always another point `Q` on the same graph which forms a segment with the fixed midpoint `M(m,n)`, meaning that `M` is the rotational center of the cubic graph.
