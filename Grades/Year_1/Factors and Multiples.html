FACTORS AND MULTIPLES

  • Factors are numbers we can multiply together to get another number.
  • Example: `2` and `3` are factors of `6`, because `2 xx 3 = 6`. A number can have many factors.
  • The multiples of a whole number are found by taking the product of any counting number and that whole number. The Least Common Multiple (LCM) of a set of whole numbers is the smallest multiple common to all whole numbers in the set. The highest common factor is the biggest multiple common to all whole numbers in the set.
  • Difference between a factor and a multiple: Factors are what we can multiply to get the number. Multiples are what we get after multiplying the number by an integer (not a fraction).

  • Use prime factorisation to find the square roots and cube roots of numbers.
    • Prime Factorisation
  • Prime factorisation is finding the factors of a number that are prime.
  • Prime numbers are numbers that can only divided by 1 and themselves.
  • For example, the prime factors of `50 = 2 xx 5 xx 5`.
    • Highest Common Factor
  • Highest common factor of `a` and `b` is donated by `(a,b)`.
  • Here are the list of prime factors of `24` and `36`:
  • `24 = 2 xx 2 xx 2 xx 3`
  • `36 = 2 xx 2 xx 3 xx 3`
  • If we write down the numbers that are the same in both lists, they will give us the highest common factor of `24` and `36`.
    `:.(24,36)=2 xx 2 xx 3 = 12`
    • `***`Remember last topic we talked about rational numbers? To be more accurate, a rational number can be expressed in the way `p/q`, where `p` and `q` are both integers and `(p,q)=1`. The second condition may not seem important, but without it, it would be very difficult to prove some questions! (To read more about this, go on to Further Readings!)
    Lowest Common Multiple
  • The Lowest Common Multiple of `a` and `b` is donated by `[a,b]`.
  • To find the least common multiple of numbers is to first list the prime factors of each number.
    `30 = 2 xx 3 xx 5`
    `45 = 3 xx 3 xx 5`
  • Next, multiply each factor the greatest number of times it occurs in either number. If the same factor occurs more than once in both numbers, you multiply the factor the greatest number of times it occurs.
  • So, the LCM of 30 and 45 would be calculated by
    `2 xx 3 xx 3 xx 5 = 90`
    • `***[a,b]=ab-:(a,b)``
