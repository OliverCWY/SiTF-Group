Real Numbers and Approximation

  • Real numbers are a list of different types of numbers that can be classified into groups
  • An approximation is anything that is similar, but not exactly equal, to something else. A number can be approximated by rounding. A calculation can be approximated by rounding the values within it.
Learning Objectives
  • Define and identify rational,irrational and real numbers and give examples of each category of numbers.
  • Round off numbers and measures to a sprecified degree of accuracy.
    • Real Numbers
  • Natural Numbers consist of positive integers. Natural numbers are usually known as counting numbers. E.g. `1,2,3,4,5,6.......`
  • Whole Numbers is a member of the set of positive integers and 0. E.g. `0,1,2,3,4,5,6......`
  • Prime Numbers are natural numbers greater than 1 and have only 2 factors: 1 and itself. Natural numbers that are greater than 1 which are not prime are called composite numbers. E.g. `2,3,5,7,11,13`
  • Integers consist of natural numbers, their negative and the number 0. E.g. `-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,4.....`
  • Fractions are an expression of the form a/b where a and b are integers and b is not equal to 0. E.g. `1/3 , 2/5`
  • Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as the ratio of two integers, a and b, in the form a/b where b is not equal to 0. E.g. `1/8, 1/3 and 4`
  • Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed in the form of a/b for any integers a and b. Irrational numbers have decimal expansions that never terminate and never repeat. E.g. `pi`
    • Approximation
  • There are five rules that you need to remeber and these are very important.
  • All non-zero digits are significant
  • A zero or zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  • In a decimal, all zeros after a non-zero digit are significant
  • In a decimal, all zeros before a non-zero digit are not significant
  • In whole number, the zeros at the end may or may not be significant. It depends on how the numbers are approximated
